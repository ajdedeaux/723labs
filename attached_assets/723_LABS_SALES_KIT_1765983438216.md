## Section 1: Founder Story — The Fresh Out Framework

### From Constraint to Capability

In April 2023, I walked out of federal prison after nine years. No network. No resume. No clue how much the world had changed.

Within 18 months:
- I became the #1 Sleep Expert in my Mattress Firm district, generating $1.2M+ in annual sales
- I landed a junior consultant role at Analytics AIML, an AI strategy firm
- I built a complete business operating system for a catering company — while working a full-time W-2

**The Chef G Events project you see here? I built it in my off-hours.** Twelve pages, six API integrations, an AI assistant, a marketing strategy based on analyzing 161 real leads, and documentation that rivals what agencies charge $50K to produce.

### How I Learned

I didn't go to bootcamp. I didn't hire a coach. I learned by doing.

My uncle Frank Shines — Lean Six Sigma Black Belt, enterprise consultant, the guy Fortune 500 companies call when systems are broken — became my mentor. He taught me to see business as systems. Inputs, outputs, constraints, leverage points.

But the real accelerator was AI. I realized that what took developers weeks, I could build in days. What took marketers months of testing, I could analyze in hours. The constraint of having no formal training became an advantage — I had no "right way" to unlearn. I just used whatever worked.

### Why 723 Labs

The name isn't random. 7/23 marks a turning point — a date that changed everything. It's a reminder that transformation happens on specific days, through specific decisions.

**723 Labs exists because I know what it's like to rebuild from zero.** Small business owners don't need another agency that charges $50K and takes six months. They need someone who understands constraint. Who builds fast because they have to. Who delivers results because reputation is everything when you're starting over.

I transformed my own life using the same methodology I now use for clients:
- Identify the outcome
- Map the system
- Leverage AI for the 80% that doesn't need human touch
- Focus human expertise on the 20% that does
- Document everything so the system runs without you

**That's the Fresh Out Framework.** It's not about where you start. It's about how fast you learn when failure isn't an option.

### Why Clients Trust Me

I don't hide my story. I lead with it.

When a client asks "Why should I trust you?" — the answer is simple: Look at what I built. Look at the results. And understand that I built this while working 50+ hours a week at a day job, with no team, no funding, and no safety net.

If I can build this under those constraints, imagine what I can do with your business.

## Section 4: The Bench — Freelancer Network Model

### Operating Philosophy

723 Labs operates lean by design. No overhead. No retainers. No employees sitting around waiting for projects.

**The model:**
- **Core:** AJ as strategist, architect, and client lead
- **Engine:** Replit as AI-augmented build platform
- **Specialists:** On-call freelancers for the 15-20% AI can't handle

This isn't about being cheap. It's about being efficient. Clients get agency-level talent without agency-level cost — because we only deploy specialists when specialists are needed.

### Core Team

| Role | Who | Responsibility |
|------|-----|----------------|
| **Lead Strategist & Architect** | AJ (Founder) | Client relationships, system design, AI integration, quality control |
| **Build Platform** | Replit + AI | 80% of development, documentation, iteration |

### On-Call Specialists

| Role | When Deployed | Typical Cost | What They Do |
|------|---------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Video Editor** | Client has raw footage, needs polished ads | $50-150/video | Edit raw clips into ad-ready content, add text overlays, optimize for platform |
| **Graphic Designer** | Custom illustrations, complex brand work | $30-75/hour | Logo refinement, custom graphics, print materials, brand extensions |
| **Ad Buyer/Manager** | Client wants managed campaigns | 10-15% of ad spend | Campaign setup, daily monitoring, optimization, reporting |
| **Copywriter** | Large content volume beyond AI capability | $0.10-0.25/word | Long-form content, email sequences, sales pages |
| **Developer** | Complex integrations, custom features | $50-100/hour | Specialty work beyond standard stack |

### Why This Works

**For Clients:**
- Pay only for what you need
- Access to specialists without retainer overhead
- Faster delivery (no internal bureaucracy)
- Single point of contact (me) regardless of team size

**For 723 Labs:**
- Zero fixed costs
- Scale up or down instantly
- Best talent for each specific need
- Margin preserved on every project

**For Specialists:**
- Consistent project flow
- Clear briefs (I do the client translation)
- Fair pay, quick turnaround
- No scope creep (I manage the client)

### The AI Advantage

Here's the real leverage: AI handles 80% of what used to require human specialists.

| Traditional Need | 723 Labs Solution |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Web developer | Replit + AI builds full-stack apps |
| Copywriter | AI generates 90% of copy, human polishes |
| Marketing strategist | AI analyzes data, I interpret and strategize |
| Designer | AI + templates for most needs, human for custom |
| Project manager | I manage directly, no overhead layer |

**Result:** A $50K agency engagement becomes a $15K 723 Labs engagement — with the same or better deliverables.

### When I Bring in The Bench

- **Video Editor:** When client has raw event footage and wants professional ads
- **Graphic Designer:** When brand needs custom illustration or complex visual work
- **Ad Buyer:** When client wants fully managed campaigns (not just strategy)
- **Copywriter:** When project requires 10,000+ words of original content

Most projects complete without any bench deployment. That's the point.

## Section 5: Sales Conversation Framework

### The 30-Second Opener

*When someone asks "What do you do?"*

> "I build business operating systems for service companies. Not just websites — complete systems that capture leads, qualify them automatically, collect payments, and give you a real marketing strategy. Think what it would cost to hire a web agency, an AI vendor, and a marketing firm — I deliver all of that in one engagement at a fraction of the cost. Most of my clients see ROI in 60-90 days."

**Key points to hit:**
- Operating systems, not websites
- Service companies specifically
- All-in-one vs. multiple vendors
- ROI timeline (60-90 days)

### Discovery Questions

*Before pitching anything, understand their situation:*

**Business Understanding:**
- "Tell me about your business — what do you do and who do you serve?"
- "How do most of your customers find you right now?"
- "What's your average sale worth?"
- "How many leads do you get in a typical month?"

**Pain Point Discovery:**
- "What happens when a lead comes in? Walk me through the process."
- "How long does it typically take to respond to a new inquiry?"
- "What's the biggest bottleneck in your sales process right now?"
- "Have you worked with agencies or developers before? How did that go?"

**Goal Setting:**
- "If I could fix one thing about your lead-to-sale process, what would it be?"
- "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"
- "What would doubling your leads mean for your business?"

### Problem Agitation

*Once you understand their pain, make it tangible:*

> "So let me make sure I understand — you're getting leads through [Instagram DMs / word of mouth / random channels], and every time someone reaches out, you have to personally respond. Sometimes that's same-day, sometimes it takes a couple days depending on how busy you are. And you're tracking all of this in [your head / text messages / a spreadsheet]?"

*Then:*

> "How many leads do you think you've lost just because you couldn't respond fast enough? Or because there was no follow-up system?"

*Let them answer. The silence is important.*

> "That's the real cost of not having a system. It's not just the leads you lose — it's the leads you never even knew you had."

### Solution Positioning

*Now present 723 Labs as the answer:*

> "Here's what I'd build for you: A complete system that captures every lead automatically, qualifies them while you sleep using an AI assistant, and routes the real opportunities directly to your phone. Your website would actually convert — not just sit there looking pretty. And you'd have a real marketing strategy based on actual data, not guesswork."

> "I just finished a project for a catering company in Sacramento. Same situation — great service, lots of referrals, but no system. In 6 weeks, we built everything: 12-page website, AI customer service rep, automated booking, payment collection, and a complete marketing playbook. They went from 'I'm too busy to take more clients' to 'my system handles the front end so I can focus on delivery.'"

> "That's what I do. I build the machine so you can focus on the work."

### Objection Handling

**"I just need a website."**

> "I hear that a lot. And honestly, if all you need is a pretty page, there are cheaper options. But let me ask — what's the point of a website if it doesn't bring in business? A website without a lead capture system, without follow-up automation, without a strategy to drive traffic... that's just a digital brochure. I build systems that pay for themselves. If that's not what you need, I'm not the right fit."

**"That's more than I wanted to spend."**

> "I get it. Let me show you the math. [Reference their average ticket and close rate.] If my system brings in just 10 extra leads a month and you close 2 of them — that's [$X] in new revenue. The system pays for itself in [60-90 days]. After that, it's pure profit. This isn't an expense. It's an investment with a measurable return."

**"I can build this myself with [Squarespace / Wix / WordPress]."**

> "You absolutely could. And if you have the time to learn the platforms, figure out the integrations, write all the copy, set up the payment processing, build the forms, connect the CRM, create a marketing strategy, and then maintain it all — go for it. Most business owners I work with tried that. They spent 6 months, got frustrated, and ended up with something that doesn't convert. I can have you up and running in 6 weeks with a system that actually works. What's your time worth?"

**"How do I know this will work?"**

> "Look at what I built for Chef G Events. [Show case study.] This isn't a concept — it's a proven system with real results. And I'm not asking you to trust me blindly. We start with a deposit, I deliver milestones, you approve each phase before we move forward. If what I build doesn't meet the spec, you don't pay the balance. I eat the risk."

**"I need to think about it."**

> "Of course. Let me send you the case study and a summary of what we discussed. But let me ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the timeline, whether this is the right solution? If I can answer something now, I'd rather do that than have you sitting on a question for a week."

### The Close

*Move to next steps clearly:*

> "Here's what I'd suggest: Let's do a discovery call — 45 minutes, no charge. I'll dig into your business, your current systems, your goals. At the end, I'll tell you exactly what I'd build and what it would cost. If it makes sense, we move forward. If not, you walk away with clarity on what you need — even if you build it yourself or hire someone else. Fair?"

*If they're ready now:*

> "Great. I'll send over a proposal within 48 hours. It'll include the scope, timeline, investment, and payment schedule. If it looks good, we sign and get started. I typically need 50% deposit to begin. Sound good?"

### Key Principles

1. **Listen more than you talk.** The best sales conversations are 70% them, 30% you.
2. **Anchor on value, not price.** Always tie the investment to ROI.
3. **Use the Chef G story.** Real results beat theoretical benefits.
4. **Qualify hard.** Not every business is a fit. It's okay to say no.
5. **Create urgency without pressure.** "My calendar fills up — if you want to start in January, we should lock in this week."
